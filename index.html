What the product does

Make comparative philosophy tractable for real research workflows

The product vision is not a quote generator. It is a retrieval system for scholars who need semantically adjacent passages, explicit provenance, and concept-level explanations across languages, traditions, and genres.

Live model

Query the corpus and inspect the counterpart logic

The browser prototype uses hand-tuned concept vectors to emulate multilingual embedding behavior. The interaction model is the important part: ask a research question, rank a canon, then inspect the matched passages and concept graph.

Architecture

How the production system scales beyond this prototype

The long-term build combines multilingual embeddings, a knowledge graph, provenance-aware retrieval, and LLM-generated comparative notes that remain subordinate to the source passages.

Research workflow

From open-ended question to interpretable parallel

Each result should return its source, activated concepts, and a ranked counterpart so scholars can inspect the relation rather than trust an opaque summary.

Research uses

Why this engine matters for academic teams

Comparative philosophy becomes much easier to scope when the system can expose semantically similar passages, preserve original language, and keep the difference between traditions visible instead of collapsing them into generic themes.
